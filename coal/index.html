Notes

Bar height is logarithmically scaled (symlog). Scroll to zoom, drag to pan. Hover a county to see exact values. Production is measured in short tons. Wyoming's Campbell County (Powder River Basin) dominates national output. No data is present for Alaska and Hawaii. Only mines reported to MSHA as coal mines are included; metal and nonmetal mines are excluded.

Data Sources

Production values are drawn from MSHA's annual Employment and Production flat files, which compile coal production data submitted by mine operators on MSHA Form 7000-2 (Quarterly Mine Employment and Coal Production Report). Data covers all coal mines in the contiguous United States from 2000 onward. Production is reported per mine subunit and aggregated here to county level using the county FIPS codes in MSHA's Mines dataset.
